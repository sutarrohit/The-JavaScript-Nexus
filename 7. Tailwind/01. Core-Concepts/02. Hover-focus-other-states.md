# Tailwind — Variants, States & How to Use Them

**What variants do:**
Tailwind applies utility classes conditionally by prefixing them with a _variant_ (e.g. `hover:`, `dark:`, `md:`). Variants let you target pseudo-classes, pseudo-elements, media/feature queries, attributes, sibling/parent states, child selectors, and custom selectors — so you can style based on interaction, structure, environment, or custom conditions without extra JavaScript.

---

## Common use-cases & examples

-   **Hover / focus / active**
    `hover:bg-sky-700`, `focus:outline-2`, `active:bg-violet-700` — style interactive states.

-   **Stacked variants**
    Combine multiple variants: `dark:md:hover:bg-fuchsia-600`.

-   **Structural pseudo-classes**
    `first:`, `last:`, `odd:`, `even:` for list/table styling (e.g. alternating rows).

-   **Form states**
    `required:`, `invalid:`, `disabled:` let the browser control styling of inputs.

-   **:has() and group/peer**
    `has-*` and `group-*` / `peer-*` let you style an element based on descendants or sibling state (great for complex components without JS).

-   **not-**
    `not-focus:hover:` — apply styles only when a condition is _not_ true.

-   **Pseudo-elements**
    `before:`, `after:`, `placeholder:`, `selection:` — style generated content, placeholders, or text selection.

---

## Media & feature queries

-   **Responsive**: `sm:`, `md:`, `lg:` for breakpoints.
-   **Container queries**: `@md`, `@lg` to respond to parent size.
-   **User preferences**: `dark:`, `motion-reduce:`, `motion-safe:`, `contrast-more:` etc.
-   **Capabilities**: `supports-[...]`, `forced-colors`, `pointer-fine/coarse`, `portrait/landscape`, `print`.

---

## Attributes & accessibility

-   **ARIA**: `aria-checked:bg-sky-700` — style by ARIA attribute values.
-   **Data attributes**: `data-[size=large]:p-8` or custom `data-*` variants.
-   **RTL/LTR**: `rtl:` and `ltr:` for multi-directional layouts.
-   **Open/inert**: `open:`, `inert:` for `<details>`, `<dialog>`, and inert sections.

---

## Child & descendant selectors

-   `*:` targets **direct children** (useful when you can't modify child markup).
-   `**:` targets **all descendants**. Note: styles applied by the parent with these selectors may be hard for children to override.

---

## Arbitrary & custom variants

-   **Arbitrary variants**: write custom selectors in `[...]`, e.g. `[&.is-dragging]:cursor-grabbing` or `[&_p]:mt-4`.
-   **Custom registered variants**: define reusable selectors in CSS with `@custom-variant` and then use `theme-midnight:...` style variants.

---

## Practical tips

-   Prefer real DOM elements to pseudo-elements when possible — easier to read and maintain.
-   Use `group` / `peer` (or named groups/peers) to avoid JS for inter-dependent styles (hover on parent affects child, sibling-driven UI, etc.).
-   Use `motion-safe`/`motion-reduce` to respect user preferences — `motion-safe` often results in less “undo” work.
-   Keep accessibility in mind: ARIA/data variants are powerful for stateful UI and assistive tech.
-   When reusing complex arbitrary selectors, register a custom variant to avoid duplication.

---

## One-line summary

Tailwind’s variant system lets you declaratively style almost any state or environment (interaction, structure, media query, attribute, or custom selector) directly in your markup — enabling rich, accessible, and responsive UIs with very little JavaScript.
